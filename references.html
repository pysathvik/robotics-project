References & Sources

A comprehensive collection of authoritative sources, academic publications, and industry resources used in creating this educational website on robotics.

About Our Sources

This educational resource draws upon a wide range of authoritative sources to provide accurate, up-to-date information about robotics technology and its applications. Our references include official reports from international organizations, peer-reviewed academic publications, industry analyses from respected consulting firms, and documentation from leading robotics companies.

We encourage readers to explore these sources for deeper understanding. The field of robotics evolves rapidly, so we recommend checking these organizations' latest publications for the most current statistics and developments.

📚 Citation Note: This resource is intended for educational purposes. All statistics and claims are sourced from the references listed below. Last updated: 2024.

Books

Recommended Books

Essential reading for understanding robotics history, technology, and future implications.

"Robot: Mere Machine to Transcendent Mind"

Author: Hans Moravec
A pioneering exploration of robot evolution and the future of artificial intelligence by a CMU robotics researcher.

"The New Breed"

Author: Kate Darling
MIT researcher explores our relationship with robots through the lens of how we've interacted with animals throughout history.

"Flesh and Machines"

Author: Rodney Brooks
Former MIT AI Lab director discusses the future of robots and their integration with human society.

"Robotics: A Very Short Introduction"

Author: Alan Winfield
Accessible overview of robotics science, technology, and societal implications from Oxford University Press.

"Introduction to Autonomous Mobile Robots"

Authors: Siegwart, Nourbakhsh, Scaramuzza
Comprehensive textbook covering the fundamentals of mobile robotics from MIT Press.

"Human + Machine"

Authors: Paul Daugherty, H. James Wilson
Accenture research on how AI and robotics are reimagining business processes and human work.
